Artificial Life?

In the 1950s, a mathmetician, Nils Aall Barricelli, created the first forms of artificial life, using mathmatics and early computing technology to run digital experiments using the rules of evolution and symbiogenesis. Presented here is a replication of his early work, based on work by Alexander R. Galloway.
As Galloway explains,
Each swatch of textured color within the image indicates a different bionumeric organism. Borders between color fields mean that an organism has perished, been borne, mutated, or otherwise evolved into something new.
